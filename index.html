What is CGPA?

CGPA stands for Cumulative Grade Point Average. It is a standard method used by educational institutions to measure a student's overall academic performance.

Unlike a simple average of grades, CGPA takes into account the credit hours assigned to each course, giving more weight to courses with higher credits.

CGPA is typically measured on a scale of 0.0 to 4.0 or 0.0 to 10.0, depending on the educational system.

How is CGPA Calculated?

1

Assign Grade Points

Each letter grade is assigned a grade point: O = 4.0, A+ = 3.5, A = 3.0, B+ = 2.5, B = 2.0, C = 1.5, D = 1.0, F = 0.0

2

Calculate Course Points

Multiply the grade point of each course by its credit hours.

3

Sum Total Points

Add up all the points from each course.

4

Divide by Total Credits

Divide the total points by the total number of credit hours.

CGPA Formula:

CGPA = Σ(Grade Points × Credit Hours) / Σ(Credit Hours)

Example Calculation:

Course Credit Hours Grade Grade Points Points (Grade Points × Credits)
Mathematics 4 A+ 3.5 14.0
Physics 4 A 3.0 12.0
C++ Programming 4 A+ 3.5 14.0
English 4 A 3.0 12.0
Aptitude 1 O 4.0 4.0
Web development 4 A+ 3.5 14.0
DSD 4 O 4.0 16.0
Total 25 86.0

CGPA = 86.0 ÷ 25 = 3.44
