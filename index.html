What is CGPA?

CGPA stands for Cumulative Grade Point Average. It is a standard method used by educational institutions to measure a student's overall academic performance.

Unlike a simple average of grades, CGPA takes into account the credit hours assigned to each course, giving more weight to courses with higher credits.

CGPA is typically measured on a scale of 0.0 to 4.0 or 0.0 to 10.0, depending on the educational system.

How is Your CGPA Computed?

Your CGPA, or Cumulative Grade Point Average, is a weighted metric that reflects your academic performance across all subjects. It is calculated by assigning point values to grades based on a fixed scale, multiplying them with the respective credit hours of each subject, and then dividing the total weighted score by the total number of credit hours. This method ensures that courses with higher credit impact your CGPA more significantly than others. Below is a practical breakdown to illustrate how CGPA is derived in a real-world scenario.
